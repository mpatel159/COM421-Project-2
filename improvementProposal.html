UI Improvement Proposal

Background

The IIT Registration portal is used by students every semester. While it accomplishes its tasks, we believe the portal can benefit from some much needed improvements. To see if other students feel the same way, we followed the following protocol in creating our usability audit:

  1. We Created a google survey asking students about the various components in the registration portal. (all questions were likert questions)
  2. We took notes on two students as they attempted to register for classes

Results From Usability Audit

The results of the google survey told us that most students were dissatisfied with the error message that comes up when something goes wrong. When we observed the two students, we also came to the same conclusion as they got frustrated trying to figure out why they were getting errors.

Potential Improvements

Several improvements can be made to the error component. If it was switched from a notification to an error bar, it would allow for cleaner styling and it would make it easier to read. On top of this change, we also recommend the error message gets reformatted. Figure 1 showcases what the current error message looks like. We recommend removing the html tags as they add too much to the message and can be distracting. On top of this, the "click here for more information" does not yield new information. All it does is move the error message from the notification down into the summary section. Figure 2 showcases a mockup of our suggestions, here the error message has been simplified and made easier to read. On top of this, the "click here for more information" is an actual URL that opens a new page in a new tab with more details about the specific error.
